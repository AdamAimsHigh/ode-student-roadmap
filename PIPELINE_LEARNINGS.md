## Unit 17 Retrospective

- **Unit 17 ("Boundary Value Problems and Sturm–Liouville Theory"): SUCCESS.** Authored `scripts/unit17_data.json` (6 modules: IVP-vs-BVP, two-point BVPs, eigenvalues/eigenfunctions, the shooting method, orthogonal functions, Sturm–Liouville theory) and rendered all 3 assets via `render_latex.py --unit 17 --mode cheat|practice|master`. All three PDFs compiled to `app/assets/pdfs/` (Cheat 91.7 KiB, Practice 58.3 KiB, Reference 118.1 KiB — each larger than its Unit 16 counterpart), no aux/log debris at root.
- **The Orthogonality Pivot (executed):** The MethodBox in 17.6 is the *proof* of eigenfunction orthogonality — two eigenpairs → multiply/subtract → Lagrange identity $y_n(py_m')'-y_m(py_n')'=\tfrac{d}{dx}[p(y_n y_m'-y_m y_n')]$ → integrate → boundary bracket vanishes under separated BCs → $\int_a^b y_n y_m w\,dx=0$. The "TheoryBox (Functional Basis)" was realized as a **formulabox** (`Functional Basis — Generalized Fourier Expansion`, $f=\sum c_n y_n$, $c_n=\langle f,y_n\rangle_w/\langle y_n,y_n\rangle_w$), reconfirming the Unit 15 finding that the 6-box style harness has no dedicated TheoryBox. The capstone worked example (17.5) is the vibrating string with fixed ends: operator/BVP → eigenpairs $\lambda_n=(n\pi/L)^2,\ \sin(n\pi x/L)$ → coefficient formula from orthogonality → **fully expanded** integration of $f(x)=x$ (denominator $L/2$ by half-angle; numerator by parts with boundary term $(-1)^{n+1}L^2/(n\pi)$ and the residual $\cos$ integral shown to vanish) → $x=\sum(-1)^{n+1}\tfrac{2L}{n\pi}\sin\tfrac{n\pi x}{L}$. No coefficient algebra skipped.
- **Friction Point (NEW — closing-bracket in box titles):** An examplebox title containing `$[0,\pi]$` failed to compile (`! Missing $ inserted`): the literal `]` inside the title closed the tcolorbox optional-argument bracket `[...]` prematurely. The Cheat Sheet and Practice Set built fine because the cheat mode drops worked examples and the practice problems put intervals in `enumerate` items (body text), not in box titles. *Fix:* never put a literal `]` in a box title — reworded to `$0\le x\le \pi$`. (Math `$...$` in titles is otherwise fine, as Unit 16's `$a=b=c=d=1$` title showed; the hazard is specifically `]`.)

## Unit 18 Retrospective

- **Unit 18 ("Fourier Series and Partial Differential Equations"): SUCCESS.** Authored `scripts/unit18_data.json` (6 modules: Fourier series/transform intuition, computing Fourier series, Fourier series for DEs / half-range expansions, separable PDEs, the Laplacian, the heat & wave equations) and rendered all 3 assets via `render_latex.py --unit 18 --mode cheat|practice|master`. All three compiled to `app/assets/pdfs/` via root-copy Tectonic (Cheat 94.7 KiB, Practice 62.1 KiB, Reference 124.2 KiB — each larger than its Unit 17 counterpart), no aux/log debris at root. Title follows the data store (`curriculum.json`: "Fourier Series and Partial Differential Equations") rather than the directive's shorthand "Partial Differential Equations", consistent with the standing rule to defer to the curriculum JSON.
- **The Fourier Pivot (executed):** Unit 18 is the payoff of Unit 17's orthogonality — the MethodBoxes shift from *proving* orthogonality to *using* it: 18.2 computes Euler–Fourier coefficients by orthogonality, 18.3 builds half-range sine/cosine expansions, and 18.4/18.6 run separation of variables so the spatial ODE is exactly the Unit 17 eigenvalue BVP and the initial data is matched by a Fourier series. The capstone worked example (18.6) is the 1D heat equation on a rod with $u(x,0)=100$: separate → eigenpairs $\lambda_n=(n\pi/L)^2,\ \sin(n\pi x/L)$ → decaying time factor $e^{-\alpha^2(n\pi/L)^2 t}$ → coefficients from the half-range sine integral ($b_n=400/(n\pi)$ for odd $n$, $0$ for even) → full series with the $t\to\infty$ decay noted. No coefficient algebra skipped. As in Units 15/17, there is no dedicated TheoryBox in the 6-box harness; the Fourier-transform "theory" (18.1) and the classical-equation classification render as **formulaboxes**.
- **Friction Point (Overfull \hbox in multi-clause display rows):** The renderer forces every formula body and every example/solution step into a single non-breaking `\[...\]` row, so bodies that pack two clauses with `\qquad` plus trailing prose overflowed badly (worst offenders 144–275pt). *Fix:* in the JSON, split multi-clause display bodies into a centered two-line `\begin{gathered} clause1 \\ clause2 \end{gathered}` (works inside both `display()` and the `\textcolor{workpurple}{...}` wrapper of `purple_display()`), and trim prose-heavy example steps. This dropped every overfull below ~60pt — the same minor single-row residual baseline as Units 13–17. Reconfirms the Unit 16 guidance ("break derivations into smaller blocks"); `gathered` is the cleanest data-only realization of it.
- **`grep`/`tail` gotcha:** the first compile pass only showed one overfull per file because the check used `tail -5`; the warnings were always there. Always `grep -iE "Overfull" | sort -u` across the full Tectonic stderr to see the true count (Tectonic runs multiple passes, so each warning repeats per pass).
